KS3 Full Course

Year 7 Topics by Term

Click each term to expand the full list of learning objectives.

🆘 Autumn Term⌄

Sequences

  • Recognise and extend number sequences formed by counting in steps of constant size
  • Generate terms of a simple sequence from a given rule
  • Generate sequences from practical contexts and describe the general term

Place Value

  • Read and write whole numbers in figures and words
  • Use decimal notation for tenths and hundredths — know what each digit represents
  • Multiply and divide integers and decimals by 10, 100, and 1,000

Mental and Written Arithmetic

  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide integers
  • Order positive and negative integers in ascending or descending order
  • Calculate temperature rises and falls across 0 °C
  • Solve word problems involving money and time

Coordinates

  • Read and plot coordinates in the first quadrant

Data Handling and Graphs

  • Represent and interpret data in line graphs, pictograms, frequency tables, and bar charts

Introducing Algebra

  • Use letters to represent unknown numbers or variables
  • Set up simple algebraic expressions from word problems

Simplifying

  • Collect like terms to simplify linear expressions
  • Multiply a single term over a bracket
🌻 Spring Term⌄

BIDMAS and Calculations

  • Recall times tables up to 12 × 12
  • Use written methods for all four operations including long multiplication and division
  • Apply BIDMAS to complex expressions
  • Use a calculator efficiently

Negative Numbers

  • Order positive and negative numbers
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide negative numbers
  • Solve problems involving negative numbers in context

Rounding and Approximation

  • Round to the nearest 10, 100, or 1,000
  • Round decimals to 1, 2, or 3 decimal places
  • Round to significant figures
  • Estimate calculations by rounding

Fractions

  • Find equivalent fractions and simplify
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions and mixed numbers
  • Calculate a fraction of an amount
  • Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages

Percentages

  • Find 50%, 25%, 75%, 10%, 5%, and 1% without a calculator
  • Find any percentage of an amount with and without a calculator
  • Increase or decrease an amount by a percentage

Decimals

  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide decimals to 2 decimal places
  • Multiply and divide decimals by 10, 100, and 1,000
  • Solve money and multi-step word problems

Ratio and Proportion

  • Write ratios in their simplest form
  • Divide a quantity into two or three parts in a given ratio
  • Solve problems involving ratio in context (e.g. scaling recipes)
☀️ Summer Term⌄

Algebra — Solving Equations

  • Solve simple linear equations using inverse operations
  • Solve equations with unknowns on both sides and with brackets
  • Form and solve equations from word problems

Substitution

  • Substitute positive and negative numbers into expressions and formulae

Probability

  • Use the probability scale from 0 to 1
  • Find probabilities from equally likely outcomes and from experiments
  • Use Venn diagrams and list outcomes of compound events

Angles

  • Recognise, measure, and draw angles accurately
  • Find missing angles in triangles and quadrilaterals
  • Identify corresponding, alternate, and interior angles

Area and Perimeter

  • Find the area of rectangles, triangles, parallelograms, and trapeziums
  • Find the area and circumference of a circle
  • Work with compound shapes

Averages and Data

  • Calculate mean, median, mode, and range
  • Find the mean from a frequency table
  • Construct and interpret pie charts and bar charts

Transformations

  • Reflect shapes in diagonal mirror lines
  • Rotate shapes about a vertex
  • Translate shapes using column vectors
  • Enlarge shapes given a centre and scale factor

3D Shapes, Volume, and Surface Area

  • Find the volume of cuboids and prisms
  • Find the surface area of cuboids and cylinders

Multiples, Factors, and Powers

  • Find HCF and LCM
  • Write a number as a product of prime factors
  • Use laws of indices

Functions and Graphs

  • Plot linear graphs in four quadrants
  • Recognise y = mx + c and understand gradient and y-intercept
