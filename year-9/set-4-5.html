📌 Set 4 & 5 — Foundation pathway. This scheme focuses on mastering core KS3 content and building a strong foundation for GCSE Foundation tier.

Full Scheme of Work

Year 9 Set 4 & 5 — Topics by Term

🆘 Autumn Term⌄

Integers and Place Value

  • Order positive and negative integers; use symbols =, <, >, ≠
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide positive and negative numbers
  • Recall multiplication facts to 10 × 10; multiply and divide by powers of 10
  • Use brackets and the hierarchy of operations

Tables and Data Collection

  • Design and use data-collection sheets for grouped, discrete, and continuous data
  • Use inequality notation for grouped data; extract data from lists and tables
  • Work out time intervals; identify the mode from a frequency table; design two-way tables

Statistics and Sampling

  • Understand primary, secondary, quantitative, and qualitative data
  • Understand sample and population; explain why a sample may be biased

Congruent Shapes and Construction

  • Understand congruence; construct triangles satisfying SSS, SAS, ASA, and RHS

Algebra

  • Use notation and symbols correctly; collect like terms
  • Multiply algebraic terms; use index notation and laws
  • Understand the identity sign ≡

Fractions

  • Find equivalent fractions; write fractions in simplest form; order fractions
  • Convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions

Charts

  • Produce and interpret pictograms, bar charts, line graphs, histograms, and stem and leaf diagrams
  • Identify mode, smallest value, and largest value from charts

Factors, Multiples, and Primes

  • List factors and multiples; find prime factor decomposition
  • Recognise two-digit prime numbers

Sequences

  • Find the nth term of linear arithmetic sequences
  • Generate terms from nth term; solve sequence problems in real-life contexts

Decimals

  • Use decimal notation and place value; compare and order decimals
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide decimals; round to decimal places and significant figures
🌻 Spring/Summer Term⌄

Straight-Line Graphs

  • Plot y = a, x = a, y = x, y = –x; recognise y = mx + c as a straight line
  • Find the equation of a straight line; find gradients; identify parallel lines

Fractions, Decimals, and Percentages

  • Convert fluently between fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Recognise recurring decimals; order fractions, decimals, and percentages

Percentages

  • Find percentages of quantities without a calculator (50%, 25%, multiples of 10% and 5%)
  • Calculate percentage increase and decrease; solve percentage word problems

Averages and Range

  • Calculate mean, mode, median, and range from raw data and frequency tables
  • Estimate mean from grouped data; compare two distributions

Indices, Powers, and Roots

  • Find squares and cubes; use index notation; recall square roots and cube roots
  • Evaluate expressions with squares, cubes, and roots

Pythagoras' Theorem

  • Use Pythagoras' theorem in 2D; calculate hypotenuse and shorter sides

Perimeter and Area

  • Find perimeter of rectangles, triangles, parallelograms, trapezia, and compound shapes
  • Find areas using formulae; convert between metric units

Ratio and Proportion

  • Write and simplify ratios; share quantities in a given ratio
  • Use ratios for scale models, converting currencies, and mixing problems

Transformation

  • Describe and perform rotations (centre, angle, direction) and translations (column vector)
  • Understand that rotations and translations preserve length and angle (congruence)

Probability

  • Use the probability scale 0 to 1; find theoretical probabilities
  • List all outcomes for single events; work out probabilities from tables

Angles

  • Estimate, measure, and draw angles accurately
  • Find missing angles on lines, at points, in triangles, and in quadrilaterals
  • Recognise alternate, corresponding, co-interior, and opposite angles

Pie Charts

  • Construct pie charts by calculating angles; interpret pie charts

HCF and LCM

  • Find HCF and LCM using listing, Venn diagrams, and prime factors

Expanding Brackets

  • Multiply a single term over a bracket; simplify expressions with brackets
  • Factorise by taking out common factors

Index Laws

  • Use laws of indices to multiply and divide numbers in index form
  • Use the zero power; use square, cube, and power keys on a calculator
