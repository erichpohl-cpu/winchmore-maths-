📌 This is the Set 1 & 2 scheme of work — an accelerated Higher pathway covering advanced KS3 content and early GCSE Higher topics. Topics include possible success criteria and common misconceptions to watch out for.

Full Scheme of Work

Year 9 — Set 1 & 2 Topics by Term

Click each term to expand the full list of learning objectives.

🆘 Autumn Term⌄

Calculations, Checking, and Rounding

  • Add, subtract, multiply and divide decimals and whole numbers
  • Multiply or divide by any number between 0 and 1
  • Use the product rule for counting
  • Round to the nearest 10, 100, 1000; to decimal places; and to significant figures
  • Estimate answers to one- or two-step calculations using rounding to 1 significant figure

Graphs: The Basics and Real-Life Graphs

  • Identify and plot points in all four quadrants
  • Draw and interpret straight-line graphs for real-life situations (conversion graphs, fuel bills, fixed charge and cost per item)
  • Draw distance–time and velocity–time graphs
  • Use graphs to calculate unit price (gradient), average speed, distance, time, and acceleration
  • Find the coordinates of the midpoint of a line segment

Polygons, Angles, and Parallel Lines

  • Classify quadrilaterals by geometric properties; distinguish scalene, isosceles, and equilateral triangles
  • Understand 'regular' and 'irregular' as applied to polygons
  • Derive and use the angle sum in a triangle (180°) and in a quadrilateral (360°)
  • Use the exterior angle theorem for triangles
  • Use corresponding and alternate angles in parallel lines, giving reasons

Algebra: The Basics

  • Use algebraic notation and symbols correctly; know the difference between term, expression, equation, formula, and identity
  • Collect like terms; substitute positive and negative numbers into expressions
  • Use index notation and laws of indices (positive, zero, fractional, and negative powers)
  • Expand single and double brackets; factorise expressions including quadratics
  • Expand and simplify products such as (2x + 3y)(3x – y)

Elevation and Construction

  • Draw 3D shapes on isometric grids; draw and interpret front, side, and plan elevations
  • Use and interpret maps and scale drawings
  • Perform standard ruler and compass constructions: angle bisector, perpendicular bisector, perpendicular from a point
  • Construct angles of 90° and 45°

Fractions

  • Express a number as a fraction of another; find equivalent fractions and simplify
  • Convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions and mixed numbers
  • Understand unit fractions as multiplicative inverses
  • Convert a fraction to a decimal to simplify a calculation

Factors, Multiples, and Primes

  • Find the prime factor decomposition of positive integers using index notation
  • Find the LCM and HCF of two numbers using listing, Venn diagrams, and prime factors
  • Solve problems using HCF and LCM
  • Understand that prime factor decomposition of a positive integer is unique

Sequences

  • Recognise odd, even, triangular, square, cube, and Fibonacci-type sequences
  • Generate terms of a sequence using the position-to-term and term-to-term rules
  • Find and use the nth term of an arithmetic sequence
  • Use the nth term to decide if a given number is in the sequence

Setting Up, Rearranging, and Solving Equations

  • Set up simple equations from word problems
  • Solve linear equations with integer and fractional coefficients, including those with brackets and negatives
  • Solve equations with unknowns on both sides
  • Change the subject of a formula (one and two steps)
🌻 Spring Term⌄

Collecting, Representing, and Interpreting Data

  • Understand primary and secondary data sources; understand sample vs population
  • Produce and interpret composite, comparative, and dual bar charts
  • Produce and interpret pie charts and frequency polygons for grouped data
  • Construct and interpret frequency diagrams for grouped discrete data

Linear Graphs and Coordinate Geometry

  • Plot y = a, x = a, y = x, y = –x, and lines parallel to the axes
  • Identify and interpret gradient and y-intercept of y = mx + c
  • Find the equation of a straight line from a graph
  • Plot and draw straight lines with and without a table of values
  • Explore gradients of parallel and perpendicular lines
  • Interpret graphs: use gradients, find approximate solutions, identify direct proportion

Percentages

  • Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Find a percentage of a quantity and work out percentage increases and decreases
  • Calculate simple and compound interest
  • Solve reverse percentage problems
  • Use multipliers to increase or decrease by any percentage

Substitution, Rearranging Formulae, and Iteration

  • Substitute into formulae including kinematics formulae (v = u + at etc.)
  • Change the subject of a formula including cases where the subject appears on both sides
  • Simple proofs and use of the identity symbol ≡
  • Use iteration to find approximate solutions to quadratic and cubic equations

Averages and Range

  • Design and use two-way tables; calculate mean, median, mode, and range
  • Construct and interpret stem and leaf diagrams (including back-to-back)
  • Find mean, mode, median, and range from a frequency table
  • Estimate the mean from grouped data using mid-interval values

Pythagoras and Trigonometry

  • Use Pythagoras' Theorem in 2D including surd answers
  • Calculate hypotenuse and shorter sides in right-angled triangles
  • Use sine, cosine, and tangent to find angles and lengths in 2D figures
  • Find angles of elevation and depression
  • Know exact values of sin/cos for 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90° and tan for 0°–60°
☀️ Summer Term⌄

Perimeter and Area

  • Recall and use formulae for area of triangle, rectangle, trapezium, and parallelogram
  • Calculate area and perimeter of compound shapes
  • Estimate area and perimeter by rounding to 1 significant figure

Circles

  • Recall and use formulae for circumference (2πr = πd) and area (πr²)
  • Calculate arc lengths, angles, and areas of sectors
  • Find radius or diameter given area or circumference
  • Give answers in terms of π; form and solve equations involving circles

Transformations

  • Describe rotations (centre and angle), reflections (mirror line equation), and translations (column vector)
  • Reflect in lines including y = x and y = –x
  • Enlarge by positive integer, positive fractional, and negative scale factors
  • Understand that reflections, rotations, and translations preserve length and angle (congruence)
  • Describe combined transformations

Probability

  • Write probabilities as fractions, percentages, or decimals
  • Use experimental and theoretical probability including relative frequency
  • List all outcomes systematically; draw sample space diagrams
  • Know that the sum of probabilities of all outcomes is 1
  • Use 1 – p for the complement of an event

3D Shapes, Volume, and Surface Area

  • Find surface area of prisms using triangle and rectangle formulae
  • Recall and use the formula for volume of cuboids and prisms
  • Convert between metric volume measures and capacity (1 ml = 1 cm³)
  • Estimate surface area, perimeter, and volume by rounding

Standard Form

  • Convert large and small numbers into standard form and vice versa
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide numbers in standard form
  • Interpret a calculator display using standard form

Ratio and Proportion

  • Write ratios in simplest form including three-part ratios
  • Divide a quantity into two or more parts in a given ratio
  • Write a ratio as a fraction; write in the form 1:m or m:1
  • Use a ratio to find one quantity when the other is known

Construction, Loci, and Bearings

  • Construct SSS, SAS, ASA triangles; bisect a line and angle
  • Construct regions defined by distance from a point or line, or equidistant from two points
  • Find and describe regions satisfying a combination of loci, including with bearings

Cumulative Frequency and Box Plots

  • Construct and interpret cumulative frequency tables and graphs
  • Estimate frequency greater/less than a given value; find median and interquartile range
  • Interpret and produce box plots from raw data; compare two distributions

Scatter Graphs

  • Draw and interpret scatter graphs; draw a line of best fit
  • Identify outliers; distinguish positive, negative, and zero correlation
  • Understand that correlation does not imply causality
  • Use a line of best fit to interpolate and extrapolate, knowing the dangers

Histograms and Time-Series Graphs

  • Produce histograms with equal class intervals; estimate the median
  • Construct and interpret time-series graphs; comment on trends
  • Recognise patterns and relationships in bar charts, line graphs, and frequency polygons
