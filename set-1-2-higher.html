Year 11 Set 1 & 2 Higher

Final Higher GCSE year — circle theorems, vectors, proof, advanced algebra, and further trigonometry.

Home › Year 11 › Set 1 & 2 Higher
🎓 Set 1 & 2 Higher — the most challenging Higher GCSE pathway. This final year builds on everything from Year 10, adding vectors, advanced functions, and further trigonometry. Two weeks at the start of term are dedicated to mock exam preparation.

Scheme of Work

Year 11 Set 1 & 2 Higher — Topics

📊 Topics Covered⌄

Circle Theorems

  • Prove and use all six circle theorems: angle at centre, angle in semicircle, perpendicular bisector of chord, angles in same segment, alternate segment theorem, opposite angles in cyclic quadrilateral
  • Use the fact that the tangent is perpendicular to the radius; tangents from an external point are equal

Practice for Mock Exams

  • First two weeks of term dedicated to revision for mocks

Probability

  • Use experimental and theoretical probability; draw probability trees
  • Calculate conditional probability using two-way tables, trees, and Venn diagrams
  • Understand selection with and without replacement; compare relative frequencies

Graphs of Trigonometric Functions

  • Recognise, sketch, and interpret y = sin x, y = cos x, y = tan x for any angle
  • Know exact values for sin/cos/tan at 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°
  • Apply transformations –f(x), f(–x), f(x) + a, f(x + a) to trig graphs

Reciprocal and Exponential Graphs; Gradient and Area Under Graphs

  • Sketch reciprocal and exponential graphs y = kx
  • Estimate area under a curve using trapezia; estimate gradient at a point by sketching the tangent
  • Interpret gradient and area under graphs in real-life contexts (distance–time, velocity–time, price)

Changing the Subject; Algebraic Fractions; Surds; Proof

  • Rationalise the denominator; simplify, multiply, and divide algebraic fractions
  • Change the subject of complex formulae including when subject appears on both sides
  • Prove results using consecutive integers, squares, even/odd numbers
  • Use function notation; find composite and inverse functions

Circle Geometry

  • Find the equation of a tangent to a circle at a given point
  • Recognise and construct x² + y² = r²

Quadratics, Cubic Graphs, and Inequalities

  • Sketch quadratic graphs identifying roots, y-intercept, and turning point
  • Solve simultaneous equations graphically (linear/quadratic, linear/circle)
  • Solve quadratic inequalities; use iteration with converging sequences

Vectors and Geometric Proof

  • Understand and use vector notation; add and subtract vectors; multiply by a scalar
  • Find the length of a vector using Pythagoras' theorem
  • Solve geometric problems in 2D; prove points are collinear; prove lines are parallel

Multiplicative Reasoning: Direct and Inverse Proportion

  • Represent repeated proportional change using multipliers; solve compound interest and depreciation problems
  • Convert between compound measure units (speed, density, pressure)
  • Recognise and solve direct and inverse proportion problems algebraically and graphically

Further Trigonometry

  • Know and apply the sine rule, cosine rule, and area formula ½ab sin C
  • Solve 2D and 3D trigonometry problems; find the angle between a line and a plane
