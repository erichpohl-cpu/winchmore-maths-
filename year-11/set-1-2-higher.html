🎓 Set 1 & 2 Higher — the final and most challenging Higher GCSE year. Mock preparation and past-paper practice run through all three terms.

Scheme of Work

Year 11 Set 1 & 2 Higher — Topics by Term

Click each term to expand the topics covered.

🆘 Autumn Term⌄

Circle Theorems

  • Recall and draw parts of a circle (sector, tangent, chord, segment)
  • Prove and use all six circle theorems including the alternate segment theorem
  • Use the tangent–radius property and equal tangents from an external point

Probability

  • Use experimental and theoretical probability; draw probability trees
  • Use Venn diagrams with union/intersection notation; conditional probability
  • Selection with/without replacement; compare relative frequencies

Practice for Mock Exams

  • First two weeks of term dedicated to revision and mock exam preparation
🌻 Spring Term⌄

Graphs of Trigonometric Functions

  • Recognise, sketch, and interpret y = sin x, y = cos x, y = tan x for any angle
  • Know exact trig values; apply transformations to trig graphs

Reciprocal and Exponential Graphs; Gradient and Area Under Graphs

  • Sketch reciprocal and exponential graphs; estimate area under a curve using trapezia
  • Estimate gradient at a point by sketching the tangent; interpret in real-life contexts

Changing the Subject; Algebraic Fractions; Surds; Proof

  • Rationalise denominators; simplify, multiply, and divide algebraic fractions
  • Change the subject of complex formulae; prove results using consecutive integers
  • Use function notation; find composite and inverse functions

Circle Geometry

  • Find the equation of a tangent to a circle at a given point
  • Recognise and construct x² + y² = r²

Quadratics, Cubics, and Inequalities

  • Sketch quadratic graphs identifying roots, intercepts, and turning points
  • Solve quadratic inequalities; use iteration with converging sequences
☀️ Summer Term⌄

Vectors and Geometric Proof

  • Use vector notation; add, subtract, and scale vectors; find vector length
  • Solve geometric problems in 2D; prove points collinear and lines parallel

Multiplicative Reasoning: Proportion and Compound Measures

  • Solve repeated proportional change, compound interest, and depreciation problems
  • Convert compound measures (speed, density, pressure); solve direct and inverse proportion

Further Trigonometry

  • Apply the sine rule, cosine rule, and area = ½ab sin C
  • Solve 2D and 3D problems; find the angle between a line and a plane

Exam Preparation

  • Continued past-paper practice and low-stakes testing to prepare for the final exams
