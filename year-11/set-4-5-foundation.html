Scheme of Work

Year 11 Set 4 & 5 Foundation — Topics by Term

The Foundation pathway. Mock exam preparation and revision are woven through all three terms. Click each term to expand.

🆘 Autumn Term⌄

Interior and Exterior Angles of Polygons

  • Recognise and name pentagons, hexagons, heptagons, octagons, and decagons
  • Understand 'regular' and 'irregular' as applied to polygons
  • Calculate and use the sums of interior angles of polygons
  • Use the fact that the sum of exterior angles of any polygon is 360°
  • Use the fact that interior + exterior angle = 180°; identify congruent shapes by eye
  • Explain why some polygons fit together and others do not

Algebra: The Basics

  • Use notation and symbols correctly; write an expression
  • Select an expression, equation, formula, or identity from a list
  • Simplify expressions by collecting like terms; multiply two simple expressions (e.g. 2a × 3b)
  • Use index notation and index laws in algebra
  • Understand the ≠ symbol and the identity ≡ sign

Charts and Graphs

  • Plot coordinates in the first quadrant; read graph scales in multiples
  • Produce pictograms, composite and dual bar charts, bar-line and vertical line charts
  • Produce line graphs, time-series graphs, histograms (equal intervals), and stem and leaf diagrams
  • Interpret all the above; calculate total population, greatest, and least values
  • Find the mode from a stem and leaf diagram or bar chart; recognise patterns and relationships

The Averages

  • Calculate mean, mode, median, and range for discrete data
  • Find averages from frequency tables, grouped data, bar charts, and stem and leaf diagrams
  • Estimate the mean of grouped data using mid-interval values
  • Compare distributions; recognise advantages and disadvantages of each measure of average

Practice for Mock Exams

  • Dedicated revision and preparation for the autumn mock examinations
🌻 Spring Term⌄

Right-Angled Triangles: Pythagoras and Trigonometry

  • Understand, recall, and use Pythagoras' Theorem in 2D, including surd form
  • Calculate the hypotenuse and shorter sides; apply Pythagoras on a coordinate grid
  • Calculate the length of a line segment AB given pairs of points
  • Use sine, cosine, and tangent to find angles and lengths in 2D
  • Find angles of elevation and depression; know exact trig values for key angles

3D Forms and Volume

  • Identify and name common solids; sketch nets of cuboids and prisms
  • Recall and use the formula for volume of a cuboid and prism (including triangular prism)
  • Estimate volumes by rounding to 1 significant figure; convert between metric volume measures
  • Convert between volume and capacity (e.g. 1 ml = 1 cm³)

Perimeter, Area, and Volume 2: Circles, Cylinders, Cones, and Spheres

  • Identify, name, and draw parts of a circle including tangent, chord, and segment
  • Use C = 2πr = πd and A = πr²; give answers in terms of π
  • Find perimeters and areas of semicircles, quarter-circles, and composite shapes
  • Calculate arc lengths, angles, and areas of sectors
  • Find the surface area and volume of cylinders, spheres, pyramids, cones, and composite solids

Similarity and Congruence in 2D

  • Use the congruence criteria for triangles (SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS); solve angle problems
  • Identify similar shapes; understand similarity of triangles and other plane shapes

Fractions

  • Find equivalent fractions; write fractions in simplest form; order and compare fractions
  • Convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions; multiply and divide an integer by a fraction

Probability

  • Work out probabilities from Venn diagrams; use union and intersection notation
  • Compare experimental data with theoretical probabilities and relative frequencies
  • Use tree diagrams for independent and dependent events

Exam Preparation

  • Practice for the spring mock examinations; assessment of all topics seen since Year 9
☀️ Summer Term⌄

Rearranging Equations, Cubic and Reciprocal Graphs, and Simultaneous Equations

  • Know the difference between an equation and an identity; use the ≠ symbol
  • Change the subject of a formula involving squares and square roots
  • Answer 'show that' questions using consecutive integers, squares, even and odd numbers
  • Find the equation of a line through two given points; identify gradient from ax + by = c
  • Recognise, sketch, and interpret cubic and reciprocal graphs
  • Write and solve simultaneous equations (linear/linear) algebraically and graphically

Proportion

  • Recognise direct proportion from the graph form; understand the relationship y = kx
  • Understand inverse proportion: as x increases, y decreases

Constructions, Loci, and Bearings

  • Use and interpret maps and scale drawings; estimate lengths using a scale diagram
  • Use three-figure bearings to specify direction; mark and measure bearings
  • Work out the bearing of B from A given the bearing of A from B
  • Use accurate drawing to solve bearings and locus problems

Transformations I: Rotations and Translations

  • Understand that rotations are specified by a centre, angle, and direction
  • Describe and perform rotations fully; rotate a shape on or off a coordinate grid
  • Understand translations as a vector; translate a shape and describe using column vectors
  • Understand that distances and angles are preserved (congruence)

Indices and Standard Form

  • Convert large and small numbers into and out of standard form
  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide numbers in standard form
  • Interpret and enter standard form on a calculator

Final Exam Preparation

  • Intensive past-paper practice and revision for the OCR Foundation examinations
📝 Assessment: Throughout Year 11, students are assessed on all topics seen since Year 9, building towards the final OCR Foundation exams.
