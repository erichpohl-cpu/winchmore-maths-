📌 Set 1 & 2 Higher — the most advanced GCSE pathway. The full Higher specification is covered across the three terms below.

Scheme of Work

Year 10 Set 1 & 2 Higher — Topics by Term

Click each term to expand the topics covered.

🆘 Autumn Term⌄

Polygons, Angles, and Parallel Lines

  • Calculate interior and exterior angles of regular and irregular polygons
  • Use the fact that exterior angles of any polygon sum to 360°
  • Solve complex angle problems using algebra

Solving Quadratics and Simultaneous Equations

  • Factorise quadratics ax² + bx + c; use the difference of two squares
  • Solve quadratics by factorisation, completing the square, and the quadratic formula
  • Solve linear/quadratic and linear/circle simultaneous equations

3D Forms and Volume: Cylinders, Cones, and Spheres

  • Recall and use the formula for volume of a pyramid; find surface area of a pyramid
  • Use formulae for volume and surface area of spheres and cones
  • Solve problems involving frustums of cones; give answers in terms of π

Graphs: The Basics and Real-Life Graphs

  • Calculate the length of a line segment from coordinates
  • Find the equation of a line through two given points

Quadratic, Cubic, and Other Graphs

  • Recognise linear, quadratic, cubic, reciprocal, and circle graphs from their shape
  • Plot quadratic and cubic functions; find approximate solutions from graphs

Similarity and Congruence in 2D and 3D

  • Prove congruence using SSS, SAS, ASA, and RHS; prove similarity formally
  • Understand the effect of enlargement on length, area, and volume; use scale factors
🌻 Spring Term⌄

Probability

  • Use experimental and theoretical probability; draw sample space diagrams
  • Work out probabilities from Venn diagrams; use union and intersection notation
  • Understand conditional probability; draw probability trees; selection with/without replacement
  • Calculate probability of independent and dependent combined events

Set Up, Rearrange, and Solve Equations

  • Set up and solve linear equations with integer and fractional coefficients
  • Derive formulae from word problems and solve them in context

Fractions

  • Decide whether fractions convert to recurring or terminating decimals
  • Convert between recurring decimals and fractions; find reciprocals

Further Trigonometry

  • Apply Area = ½ab sin C; use the sine and cosine rules to solve 2D and 3D problems
  • Find the angle between a line and a plane; calculate the diagonal of a cuboid

Sequences

  • Continue quadratic sequences and find their nth term
  • Distinguish arithmetic and geometric sequences; work with geometric progressions

Accuracy and Bounds

  • Calculate upper and lower bounds for the four operations
  • Apply bounds to perimeter, area, and volume; use inequality notation for error bounds

Direct and Inverse Proportion

  • Recognise and interpret direct and inverse proportion graphs
  • Set up and use equations of proportionality for square, cube, and other powers
☀️ Summer Term⌄

Indices, Roots, Reciprocals, and Hierarchy of Operations

  • Use index notation including negative powers; use fractional and negative indices
  • Apply index laws; use brackets and hierarchy of operations with powers and roots

Circle Theorems

  • Prove and use all six circle theorems including the alternate segment theorem
  • Use the tangent–radius property; find missing angles with full reasons

Perimeter, Area, and Circles

  • Use C = 2πr = πd and A = πr²; calculate arc lengths and sector areas
  • Form and solve equations involving complex shapes; give answers in terms of π

Percentages

  • Solve reverse percentage problems including VAT; use percentages over 100%
  • Work with compound interest and depreciation

Standard Form and Surds

  • Convert and calculate in standard form; simplify surds (e.g. √12 = 2√3)

Inequalities

  • Solve linear inequalities and represent on a number line; use inclusive/exclusive notation

Ratio and Proportion

  • Write ratio as a linear function; use ratio for scale models and currency conversion; scale recipes

Cumulative Frequency, Box Plots, and Histograms

  • Construct and interpret histograms with unequal class widths; use frequency density
  • Estimate mean and median from histograms
