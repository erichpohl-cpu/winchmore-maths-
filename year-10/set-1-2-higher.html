📌 Set 1 & 2 Higher — the most advanced GCSE pathway. This scheme covers the complete Higher GCSE specification including circle theorems, vectors, and advanced algebra.

Scheme of Work

Year 10 Set 1 & 2 Higher — Topics

📊 Topics Covered⌄

Polygons, Angles, and Parallel Lines

  • Calculate interior and exterior angles of regular and irregular polygons
  • Use the fact that exterior angles of any polygon sum to 360°
  • Solve complex angle problems using algebra

Solving Quadratics and Simultaneous Equations

  • Factorise quadratic expressions ax² + bx + c; use the difference of two squares
  • Solve quadratics by factorisation, completing the square, and the quadratic formula
  • Solve linear/quadratic and linear/circle simultaneous equations

3D Forms: Cylinders, Cones, and Spheres

  • Find volume and surface area of cylinders, cones, pyramids, spheres, and hemispheres
  • Solve problems involving frustums using similar triangles; give answers in terms of π

Graphs: Quadratic, Cubic, Reciprocal, and Circles

  • Recognise and plot linear, quadratic, cubic, reciprocal, and circle graphs
  • Find approximate solutions to quadratic equations from graphs
  • Draw x² + y² = r² (circle centred at origin)

Similarity and Congruence in 2D and 3D

  • Prove congruence using SSS, SAS, ASA, and RHS; prove similarity formally
  • Understand area and volume scale factors for similar shapes and solids

Probability

  • Use experimental and theoretical probability; draw probability trees
  • Use Venn diagrams with union and intersection notation
  • Calculate conditional probability using two-way tables, trees, and Venn diagrams

Further Trigonometry

  • Know and apply the sine rule and cosine rule for any triangle
  • Calculate area = ½ab sin C; solve 3D trigonometry problems

Sequences

  • Find the nth term of quadratic sequences
  • Distinguish arithmetic and geometric sequences; work with geometric progressions

Accuracy and Bounds

  • Calculate upper and lower bounds; use inequality notation for error bounds

Direct and Inverse Proportion

  • Recognise and set up equations for direct and inverse proportion
  • Write statements of proportionality for square, cube, and other power relationships

Indices, Roots, and Reciprocals

  • Use fractional and negative indices; use laws of indices
  • Understand n⁰ = 1 and n⁻¹ = 1/n; solve problems using index laws

Circle Theorems

  • Prove and use all six circle theorems including the alternate segment theorem
  • Find missing angles using circle theorems, isosceles triangle properties, and tangent–radius facts

Perimeter, Area, and Circles

  • Calculate arc lengths, areas of sectors, and composite shapes
  • Form and solve equations involving complex shapes

Percentages

  • Solve reverse percentage problems; use percentages greater than 100%
  • Work with compound interest and depreciation

Standard Form and Surds

  • Convert and calculate in standard form; simplify surd expressions

Inequalities

  • Solve and represent linear inequalities; use inclusive and exclusive notation

Ratio and Proportion

  • Write ratio as a linear function; use ratio for scale models and currency conversion

Cumulative Frequency, Box Plots, and Histograms

  • Construct and interpret histograms with unequal class widths; use frequency density
  • Estimate mean and median from histograms
