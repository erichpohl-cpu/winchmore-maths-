📌 Set 4 & 5 — the Foundation GCSE pathway, building essential number, algebra, and geometry skills across three terms.

Scheme of Work

Year 10 Set 4 & 5 — Topics by Term

Click each term to expand the topics covered.

🆘 Autumn Term⌄

Properties of Shapes, Parallel Lines, and Angle Facts

  • Classify and name quadrilaterals; use angle properties of triangles and quadrilaterals
  • Find missing angles using corresponding and alternate angles; show step-by-step deduction

Equations

  • Solve linear equations with brackets and fractional coefficients; rearrange simple equations
  • Use algebra to solve angle and perimeter problems

The Averages

  • Calculate mean, mode, median, and range from raw data and frequency tables
  • Estimate mean from grouped data; compare two distributions

Real-Life Graphs

  • Use coordinates in all four quadrants; find midpoints
  • Read values from straight-line real-life graphs

Expressions and Substitution

  • Derive and substitute into formulae including squares and roots
  • Substitute positive and negative numbers into expressions

Fractions

  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide mixed number fractions; find reciprocals

Inequalities

  • Show inequalities on number lines; solve combined inequalities; use correct notation
🌻 Spring Term⌄

Sequences

  • Continue geometric and quadratic sequences; distinguish arithmetic and geometric

Perimeter and Area

  • Find area of trapezium and parallelogram; areas and perimeters of compound shapes
  • Estimate surface areas by rounding; find surface area of prisms

Scatter Graphs

  • Draw scatter graphs; identify outliers; draw line of best fit
  • Distinguish positive, negative, and zero correlation; make predictions noting reliability

Quadratic Equations: Graphs

  • Generate and plot quadratic graphs; identify line of symmetry and roots
  • Find approximate solutions; interpret quadratic graphs from real life

Proportion

  • Recognise direct proportion from graph form (y = kx); understand inverse proportion

Ratio

  • Write and simplify ratios; share quantities in a given ratio including three-part ratios
  • Use ratios for scale models, currency conversion, and mixing

Indices and Standard Form

  • Use index laws for multiplication and division; understand the zero power

Plans and Elevations

  • Draw 3D shapes on isometric grids; draw and interpret front, side, and plan elevations
☀️ Summer Term⌄

Transformations I: Rotations and Translations

  • Describe and perform rotations (centre, angle, direction) and translations (column vector)

Percentages

  • Solve reverse percentage problems; work with compound interest

Transformations II: Reflections and Enlargements

  • Reflect shapes in vertical, horizontal, and diagonal mirror lines
  • Enlarge with positive integer and fractional scale factors; find the centre of enlargement

Constructions, Loci, and Bearings

  • Construct perpendicular bisectors, angle bisectors, and angles of 90°/45°

Expanding and Factorising Single Brackets

  • Expand a single bracket; factorise by taking out common factors

Circles, Cylinders, Cones, and Spheres

  • Find perimeters and areas of composite shapes with circles; arc lengths and sector areas
  • Find surface area and volume of cylinders, spheres, pyramids, and cones

Probability

  • Find probabilities from relative frequency; list outcomes for combined events; sample space diagrams

Real-Life Graphs (Further)

  • Draw and interpret distance–time and velocity–time graphs
  • Interpret gradient as rate of change in real-life contexts
